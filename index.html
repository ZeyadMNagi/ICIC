I C I C


Our dedicated team is at the forefront of innovation, working tirelessly to transform sign language into intelligible, natural speech. By leveraging cutting-edge technology, we are committed to breaking down communication barriers and improving accessibility for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. Due to the difficulty of understanding sign language and dealing with deaf and mute people, we have made a glove that converts sign language into audible audio to facilitate the process of understanding and reduce their feeling of embarrassment to create a more inclusive world where everyone's voice can be heard and understood.

Information


Listening and speaking are natural human abilities. Unfortunately, there are many people who do not have these abilities and cannot communicate with others easily. Deaf and mute people often have specific communication problems and may communicate with others using sign language, written communication, or assistive technologies. But how many people are deafeningly silent? According to the International Federation of the Deaf and Dumb, there are about 7.5 million mute people in Egypt, which represents about 10% of the world's deaf and mute population. According to data from the World Federation of the Deaf, there are a total of 360 million people affected by deafness, including 32 million children. It is also expected that, by 2050, one in four people will experience some degree of hearing loss, with 80% of these people residing in underdeveloped countries, and they use more than 300 sign languages.
